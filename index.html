About

I began my cybersecurity journey in November 2025 at AltSchool Africa, building foundations in networking, Linux systems, cloud security, and security operations. Before this pivot, I spent several years in customer operations across fintech and enterprise services at Roqqu (crypto/fintech), Honey Bucket (US enterprise services), and Tech Studio Academy (ed-tech) working daily with tools like Zendesk, Trello, and Google Sheets to triage, document, and resolve customer issues under time pressure.

That background turned out to be closer to SOC work than it first seemed: both are about taking a stream of incoming signals tickets, alerts, complaints and calmly figuring out what's actually happening, what matters most, and who needs to know. It's part of why the transition into security operations felt like a natural next step rather than a reset.

My learning approach is hands-on and lab-driven: every project in this portfolio started as a real exercise configuring a switch, building an AWS detection pipeline, running recon against a live domain, investigating a simulated phishing campaign, or executing a controlled web attack lab to understand how attackers exploit vulnerabilities and how defenders detect and respond.. Where tools didn't cooperate (API limits, version mismatches, Cloudflare-fronted targets), I worked through it rather than around it, which is documented honestly in each project's write-up.

I'm currently focused on building toward a Level 1 SOC Analyst role: alert triage, log analysis, SIEM monitoring, and incident escalation, using the same combination of structured process and clear communication that customer operations already taught me now applied to security events instead of support tickets.
